Terminology

Don’t Mix Terminology

When you mix terminology your users might see three different terms for the same concept. Or they’ll see a term that is sometimes capitalized, sometimes not. In either case, this confuses them and impacts their customer experience and our credibility.

What also happens it that inconsistent terminology gets propagated across every piece of communication. Internal terms get externalized, or product features or names are changed in one place but not in others. It’s much harder to track down and standardize terms after products have been released into market than it is when they’re still under development.

Do Follow this One Rule

So if you keep just one rule in mind when creating and using terminology it’s this: choose one customer-facing term and use it everywhere.

Common Terminology

Terms

Here are some common terms used in Pitney Bowes products. Note how some of them are proper nouns (and so they use title caps).

Concepts

Term: Meaning:
batch A group of shipments
send/sending Broad concept for “sending shipments”
ship/shipping Narrow concept for sending an individual shipment
zero out To reset a scale to zero (was formerly “tare”)

Entities

Term: Meaning:
Customs Customs organization for U.S. or other countries
Pitney Bowes Our corporate entity — never abbreviate it to “PB”
Post Office A local branch office of the USPS
USPS U.S. Postal Service acronym

Interactions

Term: Meaning:
check Action verb for checkbox user selections
checkbox A UI control type (not “check box”)
click here Avoid if possible — use descriptive hyperlinks that match the destination page’s title
password The unique password a user defines (not “pass word”)
sign in The act of accessing a product online (not “login” or “log in”)
select Action verb for non-checkbox user selections
username The unique name or email address assigned to a user’s account (not “user name”)
