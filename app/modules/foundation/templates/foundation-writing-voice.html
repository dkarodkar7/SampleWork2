Voice

Definition

The “brand voice” defines how we talk to our customers through our products and other customer-facing communications. It’s there to help ensure that we go to market with a unified, consistently applied personality and style. This voice:

  • Puts an approachable, human face on the company
  • Reflects the broader Pitney Bowes brand and value proposition
  • Ensures that our communications and products look, feel and sound consistent
  • Should apply end-to-end, at all key touchpoints in the customer experience

Goals

We want all of our products to:

  • Deliver on our positioning: At Pitney Bowes, we deliver accuracy and precision across the connected and borderless world of commerce to help our clients create meaningful impact
  • Show a unified Pitney Bowes to our customers and to the world
  • Provide a consistent, approachable and satisfying product experience
  • Reflect our brand personality and bring its qualities to life for our customers

Personality

The Pitney Bowes brand personality has four attributes:

Smart

We communicate with the confidence and sensible style of an expert, always considerate and thought through, but never overly complex or technical. Our customers don’t have time to decipher jargon, so we keep our communications and experiences intelligent and intuitive — easy to understand and navigate.

  • Smart is: expert, straightforward, confident, intuitive
  • Smart is not: technical, dry, arrogant, oversimplified

Approachable

Living up to our reputation for trust and integrity means that we communicate with a tone that is honest, human and real. Our approach and style is genuine and accessible — always open and inclusive of our clients and each other.

  • Approachable is: genuine, conversational, grounded, human
  • Approachable is not: unrefined, overly familiar, dull, disingenuous

Ambitious

Our communications and experiences are both positive and uplifting. We focus on what can be, in addition to what is. Optimism without ambition is just hopeful, so we balance our optimistic tone with drive and determination, focusing on problem-solving. We can both dream it and deliver it.

  • Ambitious is: imaginative, refreshing, courageous, determined
  • Ambitious is not: impractical, bubbly, risky, dogmatic

Energizing

We have an energy and momentum that’s contagious. While our competitors may be satisfied to deliver information, our communications and experiences inject an energy that’s nimble, motivating and inspiring.

  • Energizing is: agile, spirited, inspiring, dynamic
  • Energizing is not: erratic, loud, whimsical, frenetic

Domains

Three writing domains inform all Pitney Bowes communications and determine how well our writing supports our brand at the product level:

  • Tone of voice: copywriting that aligns with our brand personality attributes
  • Editorial style: grammar, capitalization, usage, and point of view
  • Terminology: words and phrases used to express concepts, features and functions
