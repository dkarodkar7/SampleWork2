Lean UX

Overview

Lean UX is the practice of bringing the true nature of a product to light faster, in a collaborative and cross-functional way. It does this by focusing on building a shared understanding of the product experience that’s being designed.

In order for this to happen, three things must come together:

  • Design thinking
  • Startup principles
  • Agile delivery

Design Thinking

“Design thinking is a human-centered approach to innovation that draws from the designer’s toolkit to integrate the needs of people, the possibilities of technology, and the requirements for business success.”
— Tim Brown, president and CEO, Ideo

Design thinking:

  • Focuses on discovering customer needs
  • Validates that the problems we’re trying to solve exist
  • Ensures that the solutions we produce are a market fit

Startup Principles

Lean startup is an approach to business development that’s based on the principles of lean production, a manufacturing methodology that values a business' ability to change quickly.

Core principles:

  • Uses learning to deliver better product in every iteration
  • Measures against clearly defined Key Performance Indicators (KPI)
  • Uses a “Build, Measure & Learn” cycle, with critical emphasis on learning
  • Focuses on building minimal viable products (MVP) with an experimental mindset

Agile Delivery

Agile delivery focuses development on creating immediate value, by delivering working software to customers quickly. Product iteration is based on new learning.

Agile deliver values:

  • Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
  • Working software over comprehensive documentation
  • Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
  • Responding to change over following a plan

UX Development Tracks

Multi-track Development Process

Multi-track development is a process combining different tracks for team members serving in design and user experience roles.

Continuous Delivery Track

  • Starts with an early discovery phase to define the product challenge and create a shared client understanding
  • Includes a short 4-6 week cycle of cross-functional teamwork to elaborate upon each prioritized epic
  • Validates conceptual wireframes and prototypes with clients, users and stakeholders

Detailed Design Track

  • Designers add and elaborate upon details to create conceptual prototypes needed for development
  • Designs are delivered in a consumable format (HTML), which reduces the need for heavily documented handoffs

Agile Delivery Track

  • Dedicated designers and UX developers participate in daily scrum activities
  • UX developers integrate their front-end pieces with business logic and service layers

Assumptions Worksheet

The Assumptions Worksheet is a Lean UX method that declares assumptions that help inform decisions. Declaring your assumptions allows your team to create a common starting point. By doing this as a team, you give every team member the opportunity to voice his or her opinion on how best to solve the problem. It also reveals the team’s divergence of opinions and also exposes a broad set of possible solutions.

Business Assumptions

  1. I believe my customers have a need to .
  2. These needs can be solved with .
  3. My initial customers are (or will be) .
  4. The #1 value a customer wants to get out of my service is .
  5. The customer can also get these additional benefits .
  6. I will acquire the majority of my customers through .
  7. I will make money by .
  8. My primary competition in the market will be .
  9. We will beat them due to .
  10. My biggest product risk is .
  11. We will solve this through .
  12. What other assumptions do we have that, if proven false, will cause our business/project to fail?

User Assumptions

  1. Who is the user?
  2. Where does our product fit in his work or life?
  3. What problems does our product solve?
  4. When and how is our product used?
  5. What features are important?
  6. How should our product look and behave?

Source: Lean UX: Applying Lean Principles to Improve User Experience

Strategy Canvas

The strategy canvas is a central diagnostic tool and an action framework. It graphically captures, in one simple picture, the current strategic landscape and the future prospects.

The strategy canvas serves two purposes:

  • To capture the current state of play in the market space, clarifying factors that the industry competes on and where the competition currently invests
  • To propel users to action by reorienting their focus

Experience Maps

Experience mapping is a process of capturing and communicating complex customer interactions. The activity of mapping builds knowledge and consensus across your organization, and the map helps build seamless customer experiences.

Personas & Proto-personas

Personas are fictional yet realistic descriptions of your software’s users. In most cases, personas are synthesized from data collected from team observations of users.

Proto-personas are based on secondary research and the team's educated guess of who they should be designing for.

Personas typically include:

  • Social and demographic characteristics
  • Needs, desires, goals
  • Habits (consumer habits, behavior)
  • Expertise
  • Cultural background
  • Motivations
